Digital Public Library of America

Here is some information about the vast public resource that is the DPLA

The Digital Public Library of America is a large hub of information. A massive, free, online library for anyone to use for research of any kind. Along with access to a wide variety of sources, the site also hosts online exhibitions and primary source sets. These exhibitions and primary sources are used to tell different stories from American history.

  • The site has features that allow users to cite the sources provided. For educators, this site can be used with Google classroom. There are also teaching guides that can be used to supplement a lesson.

Who is the target audience?

The site is meant for a variety of users. Experienced researchers can use the site to gain access to public libraries and state archives from all over the country. Educators can use the exhibits and the primary source sets as free resources for their classes. For individuals who are not familiar with the research skills necessary for navigating a database of this size, there are guides that provide all the information needed so users can learn how to use it. They provide instruction on how to do a boolean search and other tips for finding sources that fit their research needs.
